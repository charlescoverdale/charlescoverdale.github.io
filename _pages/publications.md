Working papers and research articles.

## 2026

**Coverdale, C.** (2026). *yieldcurves: Yield Curve Fitting, Analysis, and Decomposition in R.* Working paper, 2026. \[[PDF](/files/coverdale_yieldcurves_2026.pdf)\] \[[code](https://github.com/charlescoverdale/yieldcurves)\]

An R package for working with government bond yield curves. Fits smooth curves to the handful of bond yields published each day, then lets you read off rates at any maturity, compute interest-rate risk, and decompose curve movements into level, slope, and curvature.

**Coverdale, C.** (2026). *inequality: Inequality Measurement, Decomposition, and Poverty Analysis in R.* Working paper, 2026. \[[PDF](/files/coverdale_inequality_2026.pdf)\] \[[code](https://github.com/charlescoverdale/inequality)\]

An R package for measuring income and wealth inequality. Computes the standard set of indices (Gini, Atkinson, Theil, Palma, and more), decomposes inequality into between- and within-group components, and covers poverty measures, tax progressivity, and redistribution. Every function accepts survey weights.

**Coverdale, C.** (2026). *predictset: Conformal Prediction and Uncertainty Quantification in R.* Working paper, 2026. \[[PDF](/files/coverdale_predictset_2026.pdf)\] \[[code](https://github.com/charlescoverdale/predictset)\]

An R package that wraps any prediction model in a calibrated layer of uncertainty. Produces prediction intervals for numerical forecasts and prediction sets for classification that are guaranteed to contain the true value at a chosen coverage rate, without any distributional assumptions. Works with linear models, random forests, xgboost, or any custom model.

**Coverdale, C.** (2026). *mpshock: An R Package for Cross-Country Monetary Policy Shock Analysis.* Working paper, 2026. \[[PDF](/files/coverdale_mpshock_2026.pdf)\] \[[code](https://github.com/charlescoverdale/mpshock)\]

An R package bundling thirteen monetary policy shock series for the United States, United Kingdom, and Australia. Each series is loaded with a single function call, carries its paper citation, and plugs directly into the standard R packages for impulse response estimation.
